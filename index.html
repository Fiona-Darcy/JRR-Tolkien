JRR Tolkien

A creator of Worlds



Tolkien's Major Works:


    • 1937 - The Hobbit, or There and Back Again

    The Hobbit was realeased to wide critical acclaim and is now one of the best selling books of all time. It tells the story of the hobbit, Bilbo Baggins, as he joins the wizard Gandalf and thirteen dwarves, displaced from their home by the dragon Smaug. What follows is a story of personal growth and adventure.



    • 1954-1955 - The Lord of the Rings

    Originally written as a single volume, publishers instead released The Lord of the Rings in three separate volumes. The series is a sequel to The Hobbit, but the tone is much darker and it is intended for an older audience. It follows many characters, but primarily concerns Frodo Baggins on his quest to destroy The One Ring and its maker, Sauron.


    Works published posthumously, by Christopher Tolkien:


  • 1977 - The Silmarillion

    • The Silmarillion was published after JRR Tolkien's death in 1973. In collaboration with Guy Gavriel Kay, Christopher Tolkien compiled pieces of his father's work to create what is essentially a history of Middle Earth.



  • 2007 - The Children of Húrin


  • 2009 - The Legend of Sigurd and Gudrún


  • 2013 - The Fall of Arthur


  • 2014 - Beowulf: A Translation and Commentary


  • 2015 - The Story of Kullervo


  • 2017 - Beren and Lúthien


  • 2018 - The Fall of Gondolin
